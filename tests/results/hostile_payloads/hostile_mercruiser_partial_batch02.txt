NOTE: ABYC Standard E-11 recommends galvanic isolators for all shore-powered boats. This is the single most important shore-power investment for drive longevity.

When connected to shore power, the boat's underwater metals can become part of a larger electrical circuit with other boats or the marina's grounding system. If there are dissimilar metals or stray electrical potentials, the drive unit can become anodic and corrode rapidly. Faulty wiring, loose connections, or improper bonding within the boat's electrical system, particularly when connected to shore power, can also create stray current paths (Source 3, 15, 25, 32, 37, 48).
